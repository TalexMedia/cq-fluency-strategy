02 The Equity You've Built

Three Strengths Already In Place

All three are earned, all three are working. Any new video would build on what's already here.

Operator equity. CQ fluency has been operating since 2000. Elisabete Miranda founded it after her own daughter nearly received the wrong treatment because of a translation error. The company has now been on the Inc. 5000 list for more than ten years running, was a 2025 Inc. 5000 honoree with 93% three-year growth, became a portfolio company of Ariel Alternatives in May 2023, and works in more than two hundred languages with offices in Hackensack, Galway, and Barcelona. The enterprise client roster includes Abbott, Merck, AbbVie, MetLife, and Humana. The credentials are clear.

Voice equity. The company has a written voice that already gives without expectation. The AEP translation guide, the MDR and IVDR language guides, the Cultural Adaptation eBook, and the CQpedia cultural intelligence encyclopedia are real free resources that procurement leads, regulatory leads, and health-plan marketers actually use. The blog runs articles with a clear point of view: pronouns and gender across languages, heart disease and Hispanic women, multicultural health engagement. CQ has earned the right to speak in this category.

Lived-experience equity. Most B2B companies have to invent a brand story; CQ already has two. Elisabete's is the founding story on the About page, the one about her daughter nearly receiving the wrong treatment because of a translation error. Tameeka's own experience, as a former Medicaid beneficiary later recognized by Fortune's Most Powerful Women: Next Generation, sits in the WBL feature and the appointment press release. Together those two accounts put CQ among the very few B2B companies whose top two leaders both have a direct, personal stake in the people the company helps reach.

The one thing missing from the public presence is long-form video that comes back week after week: a founder film, customer hero films built around CQ's enterprise clients, and a returning interview show. The work CQ already does is excellent, and our Legendeering framework is designed to translate that work into video formats your community will want to watch.

03 Lead With Value

The Legendeering Framework, In One Page

The methodology behind every Talex production. Five directives, one core principle.

Legendeering is Talex's proprietary strategy. The core principle is straightforward: lead with value, always. The brand earns trust by giving without expectation, and that trust grows over time because people keep choosing to watch.

Five directives drive it:

  • ·Give Without Expectation. Value delivered without strings. The kind of generosity that builds loyalty money cannot buy.
  • ·Be Relentlessly Authentic. Say true things. Show real work. Speak like a human being.
  • ·It's Not About You. It's About Them. The audience has to come first. It's all about their wants and needs and making something they will choose to watch.
  • ·Start With Story, Follow With Facts. Story gives information shape, context, and emotional weight. Facts alone rarely move people.
  • ·Consistency Is Key. Trust is built in moments, repeated over time.

CQ fluency are experts at culturally relevant translation and accessibility for highly regulated industries. Talex would add the long-form video: documentary films built on the work CQ is already doing for clients and the communities those clients reach. 04 Three Concepts For CQ fluency

Three Long-Form Concepts For The CQ fluency Brand

Each one builds on equity CQ fluency has already earned. Each one is something only CQ fluency could make.

01
"The First Word"
This is a returning documentary series in the spirit of Vice and Anthony Bourdain's Parts Unknown highlighting the role of culture in language and communication. Each episode tells the story of a person from the moment language stands between them and the care, coverage, or clinical trial they need to the point where they get to read, understand, and decide in their own language. This is all possible thanks, of course, to CQ's linguists, interpreters, and cultural advisors. The CQ client (a health plan, a life sciences company) is the institutional setting, and a CQ team member guides the audience through the story.
02
"Connecting The Dots, Restarted"
CQ already proved the format works. The "CQ fluency Presents | Connecting the Dots" interview series sits on the YouTube channel and just stopped a few years ago. Restart it with a new host, one health-plan CMO, regulatory lead, or health-equity researcher per episode, talking about the specific multicultural communication challenge they're facing and how language and culture sit inside it. The viewers are the same people the firm is already trying to reach, the show gives them something worth subscribing to, and every episode produces five to seven short clips that become part of your content library for years to come.
03
"The Translation Room"
This is a behind-the-scenes documentary series in the spirit of Drive to Survive and Last Chance U. Those shows put cameras inside high-stakes professional work (a Formula 1 garage, a junior college football program) and made the audience care about the people doing the job. The same approach applied here, following one high-stakes CQ translation project per episode from kickoff to delivery: a Medicare Annual Enrollment cycle for a Fortune 500 health plan, a clinical-trial consent rollout for a global pharma sponsor, the launch of multilingual member-services communications for a new health-insurance product. Each episode puts the viewer inside the room with the CQ linguists, the cultural advisors, and the client team. The drama is the actual work: the deadlines, the cultural choices that change meaning, the back-and-forth between subject-matter experts and the people translating the words. The viewer roots for the team to land the project before the deadline.
